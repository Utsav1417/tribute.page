Tribute to COVID-19 Warriors


Thank You Health Worker, for all that you do.
The last person you think of right now is you.
Thank you for all the hours you tirelessly put in.
To ensure this Covid battle we eventually win.

Thank you for facing this silent killer with such bravery and grace.
We see the sore marks, the pain it leaves on your body and face.
Thank you for the time you spend away from those you love most.
Even that you do gracefully, so modest, no boasts.

Thank you for working without the protection you deserve.
It is your life too they should seek to preserve.
The unimaginable pain of seeing your friends too get sick.
Alone with the fear you may be the next one it picks.

Just know that as each day draws to an end.
In your public you have the support of a friend.
A queue jump, a smile, a nod of the head.
All show our respect for the sweat and tears that you shed.

So one final thank you for all that you do.
Our porters, our cleaners, our ambulance crew.
Our doctors, our nurses, all our heroes in blue.
we wouldn’t be here if it wasn’t for you.

Thank you for showing the world you’re the best.
You’re our pride, you’re our joy you’re our NHS.
-Poem by Victoria Gilburn




SYMPTOMS

Common Symptoms :

  • Fever
  • Dry Cough
  • Tiredness

Some people may experience :

  • Aches and Pains
  • Diarrhoea
  • Runny Nose
  • Nasal Congestion
  • Sore Throat

For more details: WHO.int

PREVENTION OF COVID-19

  • Clean your hands often. Use soap and water, or an alcohol-based hand rub.
  • Maintain a safe distance from anyone who is coughing or sneezing.
  • Don’t touch your eyes, nose or mouth.
  • Cover your nose and mouth with your bent elbow or a tissue when you cough or sneeze.
  • Stay home if you feel unwell.
  • If you have a fever, a cough, and difficulty breathing, seek medical attention. Call in advance.
  • Follow the directions of your local health authority.
